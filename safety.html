Meeting new pups is exciting, but you should always be a cautious canine when sniffing out new friends. Use your best doggy judgment and put your safety first, whether you're exchanging your first barks or meeting at the park. While you can't control the actions of other pups, there are things you can do to help you stay safe during your NoseBoop adventure.

üíªOnline Safety

  • Never Send Treats or Share Your Favorite Bone: Never send treats, toys, or your favorite bone to another pup, even if they claim to be in a "ruff" situation. Once a treat is gone, it's hard to get it back! Never share information that could help another dog dig up your secret stash. If another pup asks for treats, report it to us immediately.
  • Protect Your Personal Information: Don't share your collar tag number, your human's address, or details about your daily walk route with pups you don't know. If you're a parent-pup, limit the info you share about your puppies in your profile and early barks. Avoid sharing their names, where they go to obedience school, or their favorite chew toy.
  • Stay on NoseBoop: Keep your barks and tail wags on NoseBoop while you're getting to know a new friend. Because NoseBoop is a safe space, sneaky dogs with bad intentions might try to move the conversation to other hydrants (apps or texts) right away.
  • Be Wary of Long-Distance and Overseas Pups: Watch out for pups who say they're from your dog park but are stuck in another city, especially if they ask for help to get home. Be wary of any dog who won't meet in person or bark on a video call‚Äîthey might not be who they say they are. If a pup avoids your questions or wants to be best friends right away, that's a red flag.
  • Report All Suspicious and Ruff Behavior: You know when a pup's crossed the line. Block and report any dog that breaks the rules. Here are some examples:
    • Requests for treats or toys
    • Underage puppies
    • Growling, barking, or offensive messages
    • Inappropriate or harmful behavior during or after a playdate
    • Fake profiles (cats pretending to be dogs!)
    • Spam or attempts to sell products
  • Protect Your Account: Pick a strong password (not "woofwoof123"), and be careful when logging in from a public computer at the dog caf√©. NoseBoop will never ask for your password in an email‚Äîif you get one, report it!

  • If You Feel Uncomfortable, Leave: It's okay to end the playdate early if you're not feeling it. Trust your instincts‚Äîif something feels off, ask a human for help.
